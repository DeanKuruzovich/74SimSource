Privacy Statement

Effective date: 2026-05-16

Who we are

74Sim is an interactive 74 series logic-chip breadboard simulator available at 74sim.com and as a desktop application.

What data we collect

  • Feedback and bug reports text you voluntarily submit through the in-app feedback or bug-report forms, plus a timestamp. No account or login is required.
  • Example circuits circuit diagrams you choose to submit for the public example library. These are stored as-submitted and may be published.
  • Server logs standard web-server request logs (IP address, user-agent, URL, timestamp) retained for up to 30 days for security and debugging.

We do not use cookies, tracking pixels, analytics services, or advertising networks. We do not collect names, emails, or any account information.

How we use data

  • Feedback and bug reports are read by the developer to improve the simulator.
  • Submitted example circuits may appear in the in-app example library.
  • Server logs are used only for diagnosing errors and detecting abuse.

Data sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share personal data with third parties, except as required by law. Submitted content is stored on DigitalOcean Spaces (S3-compatible object storage, US region).

Desktop application

The desktop version of 74Sim (Windows, macOS, Linux) communicates with 74sim.com only when you explicitly submit feedback, report a bug, or check for updates. No telemetry or background data collection occurs.

Your rights

To request deletion of any data you have submitted, contact us through the in-app Feedback or Report-a-Bug forms, or via r/74Sim, with a description of what you submitted and approximately when. We will remove it within 30 days.

Changes to this statement

If this statement changes materially, the effective date above will be updated. The current version is always at 74sim.com/privacy.html.

Contact

Use the in-app Feedback or Report-a-Bug forms, or reach us at r/74Sim.
